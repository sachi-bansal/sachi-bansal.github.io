Biosynthetic Knee Endoprosthesis

Overview

Solving Soft Tissue–to–Metal Attachment

Loss of the patellar tendon (PT) insertion during endoprosthetic knee reconstruction compromises the knee extensor mechanism, severely limiting post-surgical function. Current approaches attempt to reattach soft tissue directly to metal — an interface that remains mechanically unreliable long-term.

We propose a biosynthetic endoprosthesis that incorporates a bone block housed within the tibial component as a biological landing pad, enabling direct suturing of the patellar tendon to living tissue rather than metal.

Ex Vivo Validation

Proof-of-Concept Testing

We designed the prosthesis to house a vascularized bone block in the antero-proximal aspect of the tibial component and conducted an ex vivo dissection to verify prosthesis morphology and surgical feasibility.

The construct was mechanically tested using a KUKA KR-210 robotic manipulator under increasing load until failure, identifying the mechanical limits of each interface and informing the next design iteration.
