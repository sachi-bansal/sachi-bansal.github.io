Post-Traumatic Osteoarthritis Research

Study Overview

Preventing Joint Degeneration after ACL Injury

ACL injuries are among the most common orthopaedic traumas, frequently leading to post-traumatic osteoarthritis (PTOA) — progressive cartilage degeneration that significantly reduces quality of life. As Clinical Research Coordinator at Stanford's Department of Orthopaedic Surgery, I coordinated a DoD-funded multi-site longitudinal study evaluating interventions to prevent PTOA following ACL reconstruction.

My Role

Coordination & Analysis

I executed IRB-approved protocols across study sites using REDCap, coordinated with orthopaedic surgeons, biostatisticians, and biomedical engineers for multi-modal data collection including patient-reported outcomes, gait analysis, and longitudinal MRI imaging.

I developed semi-automated MRI segmentation algorithms in MATLAB to analyze cartilage tissue changes across over 1,400 images, correlating biomechanical loading patterns with tissue degeneration outcomes.
