I'm a biomedical engineering PhD candidate at UCLA, where I design and build prosthetic devices that sit at the intersection of engineering, medicine, and human experience. My doctoral research focuses on novel ways to connect the human body and prosthetic devices, integrating mechanical design, surgical validation, and years of collaboration with surgeons, clinicians, and engineers to create something that could genuinely restore lost joint function and improve device longevity within the body. During my PhD, I also gained industry experience as a Digital Health Intern at Samsung Research America, where I developed algorithms and conducted clinical testing for consumer wearable devices.

Before my PhD, I spent two years in clinical research at Stanford's Department of Orthopaedic Surgery, coordinating multi-site studies and building a foundation in how research actually translates to patient care. I've worked across the full pipeline — from mechanical design and animal studies to multi-site clinical trials — so I understand how every engineering decision ultimately shapes the patient experience. I'm drawn to problems that require holding both the technical and the human side in mind at once, whether that's in the lab or thinking through how a device will actually feel and function for a patient.
